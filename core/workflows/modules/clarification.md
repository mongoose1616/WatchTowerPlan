# Clarification Workflow

## Purpose
Use this workflow to reduce the specific ambiguity, conflict, or missing input that is materially blocking the active task.

## Use When
- A routed task already has a scoped clarification target and needs the ambiguity reduced or explicitly handed back to a decision-maker.
- The blocking issue is narrow enough that clarification work can focus on resolving or packaging that issue rather than re-scoping the whole task.
- The next workflow step depends on separating confirmed facts, accepted assumptions, and still-open questions cleanly.

## Inputs
- Scoped clarification brief
- Current-state context summary
- Internal standards and canonical references applied
- External guidance notes when needed
- Known assumptions, conflicts, constraints, and affected downstream workflows

## Workflow
1. Reduce the ambiguity.
   - Resolve the question directly from gathered context when the answer is already available.
   - If the ambiguity cannot be resolved from available sources, formulate the minimum clear clarification needed from the human decision-maker or requestor.
   - Separate confirmed facts from proposed assumptions and from still-open questions.
2. Record the clarification outcome.
   - State the clarified answer, accepted assumption, or outstanding question clearly.
   - Note whether the ambiguity is resolved, partially resolved, or still blocked.
   - Identify what downstream workflow should resume once clarification is complete.

## Data Structure
- Clarification target
- Blocking issue
- Confirmed facts
- Assumptions considered
- Outstanding questions
- Clarification outcome
- Downstream workflow to resume

## Outputs
- A clarified answer, confirmed assumption set, or explicit unresolved question package
- A clear statement of whether work can resume, must be rerouted, or remains blocked
- A downstream workflow restart recommendation

## Done When
- The material ambiguity has been reduced enough for the next workflow decision or execution step.
- Confirmed facts, assumptions, and still-open questions are clearly separated.
- The next workflow or decision-maker can continue without major missing context being filled in verbally.
